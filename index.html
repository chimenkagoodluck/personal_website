Who I am

About Me

I'm Chimenka Goodluck, popularly called "Cyberboy." I am a young researcher and a software engineer who develops web-based systems as well as desktop applications, and my main interest is making secure intelligent systems, which solve real problems in the society. I chose cybersecurity, AI/ML and software development as my interests because I believe these are the areas that the future of tech is heading towards. Specifically, I want to identify and address gaps that exist in the security of AI systems, and to build tools that can help organizations protect against emerging threats. I am passionate about research and development in these fields, and I am always eager to learn and grow as a researcher and problem solver.

My research focuses on adversarial robustness in machine learning, AI in cybersecurity, criminal, forensic and civil investigations as they relates to law, cybersecurity and AI. I enjoy translating academic ideas into real-world tools that developers and security teams can actually use.

When I'm not writing code or reading papers, I enjoy playing the piano or watching global news. I currently live in Lagos, Nigeria, and I am open to remote work anywhere in the world. I can also relocate for opportunities related to education, research, work, or other important commitments.

EvidenceHub: A Cross-Modal Explainable Artificial Intelligence System for Automated Digital Forensic Evidence Authentication and Traceability
N Computer Science (Springer Nature Journal)
This paper discusses the design and development of an AI-based digital evidence authentication system that can improve forensic investigations. Current methods have shown some limitations in terms of explainability, accuracy, and real-time processing, especially with the increasing number of digital forgeries, deepfake images, and metadata manipulations. This new system can address these limitations through the integration of deep learning models, natural language processing, metadata analysis, blockchain-based chain of custody monitoring, and a unified forensic online platform.

Comparative Analysis of Lightweight Cryptography and Blockchain-Based Security Protocols for Mitigating Threats and Vulnerabilities in the Internet of Things
International Journal of Research and Innovation in Applied Science 11(1), https://doi.org/10.51584/IJRIAS.2026.110100130.
In this paper, we examined how lightweight cryptography and blockchain-based security protocols can be used together to address the major threats and vulnerabilities affecting Internet of Things (IoT) devices, especially in resource-constrained environments. Using tools such as Bevywise IoT Simulator, MQTT Route, and Wireshark, we evaluated their effectiveness and showed that blockchain improves data integrity and immutability, while lightweight cryptography strengthens secure communication in IoT systems.

A Critical Assessment of the Enterprise Security Framework and Cybersecurity Posture of Clifford University Medical Center: Challenges, Risks, and Recommendations for Improvement.
Academia ·
The study looks into the difficulties brought on by CUMC's dependence on manual computation systems, limited infrastructure, poor employee training, and lax security procedures. These problems have led to poor medical care, operational bottlenecks, data manipulation vulnerabilities, and general stakeholder discontent. Online surveys, student interviews, and conversations with important medical personnel, such as nurses, lab technicians, finance managers, and receptionists, were used to collect data. The results show that the center's capacity to deliver high-quality healthcare services is jeopardized by the lack of digital systems, such as electronic health records (EHR) and safe payment methods.

Imagine this scenario: const data = fetch('https://api.example.com/users'); console.log(data); // Won’t work as expected! The fetch call is asynchronous—it returns a Promise. If we don’t handle it correctly, our code becomes unpredictable or breaks.
